# Phase 1 Revamp Audit

## SYNTHESIS

### Executive summary
The science is the spine of this app and it is genuinely strong: latest_snapshot de-pools repeat visits, cover uses a real structural-zero denominator, Chao2 is the correct incidence estimator, unknown_rate is published, and a single site_invasion recipe keeps the picker and the hero honest. What holds the app back is identity and plumbing, not math: the chrome is a byte-for-byte clone of the sibling NEON Small Mammal Tracker (identical --pine #0C234B / --terra #AB0520 / --gold #FFD200 triad, Rubik font, navy hero gradient) so the two read as one product, and the ported client still ships dead mammal code (smtTour targeting non-existent selectors, rodentConfetti with the UA palette, neon-mammal-/neon-bodysize PNG filenames, an undeclared --accent token, an unused confetti CDN). The single biggest opportunity is a centralized theme revamp to Vera's Herbarium direction that funnels through ~6 CSS vars plus a single R PAL list so the page, plotly tooltips, pin lines, and confetti all re-skin from one source of truth — and to bake that revamp around the two roadmap features so they ship clean: precompute per-site metrics into the bundle (so 46 sites and the env tab read answers, never recompute), drive every all-site view from SITE_INDEX only, and build the Environment correlation with deseasonalization + a permutation null + matched-n so the half-wired .ec banner doesn't become data-dredging. Reserve the green/terra axis exclusively for nativity and give correlation sign its own CVD-safe blue/vermillion poles before the env tab lands.

### Theme pick
Herbarium. Its warm leaf-green primary (#1F5C3D) on herbarium-cream (#F5F1E6) with a clay-terracotta accent and ochre highlight is the cleanest, most legible break from the mammal app's cool navy/cardinal/gold-on-blue-grey — it shifts hue, temperature, and mood at once while preserving the native=green / introduced=warm data semantics the whole app is built on. It hits AAA on the load-bearing text pairs (7.0:1 white-on-primary, 12.6:1 ink-on-paper), the Fraunces heading face de-couples the typography from the all-Rubik mammal feel, and it pre-solves the Environment tab by leaving the cool↔warm correlation axis (blue/vermillion) free of the chrome greens.

## SPECIALIST DETAIL

### Quinn · data-science
Quinn here — spent the afternoon in R/plant_helpers.R and the server.R renders that consume it, and tracing the bundle build in scripts/bundle_plant_data.R. The good news first: the science here is unusually honest for a Shiny app. latest_snapshot() correctly de-pools repeated yearly visits, plot_species_cover() uses a real structural-zero denominator, Chao2 is the right incidence estimator (not the count-based Chao1 the mammal app uses), the unknown_rate is published, and a single site_invasion() recipe is shared by the bundler and the hero so the picker and the headline can't disagree. That said, there are three places the numbers are quietly biased: the snapshot collapses years but NOT bouts (NEON runs multiple bouts/year), so a quadrat's spring+monsoon visits get double-counted into richness, the cover denominator, and the Chao2 incidence units; Hill diversity is computed on summed ocular cover (overlapping layers, not a real abundance, and the sum mixes bouts); and the species-area curve computes a per-area sd it then throws away, so the flagship Diversity curve ships with no uncertainty band. None are fatal, all are fixable in the helper layer, and the fixes are mechanical. The bigger opportunity is what the 46-site + env-data roadmap unlocks: cross-site beta-diversity/turnover, a proper sample-based rarefaction with CIs (iNEXT), rank-abundance/Whittaker plots, and a climate-diversity lag correlation — but the env feature MUST ship with deseasonalization, a permutation null over the lag scan, and the matched-n on screen, or it becomes data-dredging on a short series.


**Findings:**
- [high/M] **Snapshot de-pools years but not bouts — repeated within-year visits double-count** (R/plant_helpers.R latest_snapshot(); flows into chao2(), plot_species_cover(), plot_summary())
  - problem: latest_snapshot() keeps max(year) per plotID, but the bundle carries a `bout` column (bundle_plant_data.R transmutes `bout = boutNumber`) and several NEON plant sites run 2 bouts in a single year (e.g. spring + monsoon at SRER/JORN). For a plot last surveyed in a 2-bout year, the snapshot retains BOTH bouts. Every downstream metric then treats one quadrat's two visits as two independent samples: richness (n_distinct over the union of both bouts) is inflated, Chao2's incidence unit `paste(plotID, subplotID, year)` is the SAME string for both bouts so a species present in both is counted once but the unit count m is right while Q1/Q2 are distorted by the within-bout turnover, and plot_species_cover()'s n_sub = n_distinct(subplotID) counts each physical 1m2 subplot once across bouts but sums cover across both — so mean_cover = sum(2 bouts)/n_sub double-weights cover. This is exactly the pooling trap the playbook (gotcha line 115) warns about, applied one level finer than years.
  - fix: In latest_snapshot(), pick the latest (year, bout) pair per plotID, not just max(year): group_by(plotID), summarise(.snapyr = max(year)), then within that year take max(bout) — or carry a single (year,bout) key. Then assert one bout per plot in the snapshot and, in chao2()/plot_species_cover(), build the incidence unit and the n_sub denominator from `paste(plotID, subplotID, year, bout)` so a within-year resurvey can never be two samples. Add a one-line scope note on the Diversity/Overview cards stating which survey (year+bout) the snapshot is.
- [medium/M] **Hill diversity on summed ocular cover treats overlapping cover as true abundance** (R/plant_helpers.R hill_site()/hill_numbers(); server.R output$hillPlot/hillInsight)
  - problem: hill_site() builds the abundance vector as tapply(percentCover, scientificName, sum) over all 1m2 records, then feeds it to Hill numbers as if it were a count-like abundance. Two problems: (1) NEON cover is an ocular estimate and vegetation LAYERS OVERLAP — the app itself says so in the About tab and hero tooltip — so summed cover can exceed 100% and is a relative index, not a partition of a finite resource the way counts are. Inverse-Simpson (q2) and Shannon (q1) on an over-100% non-partition are still computable but their 'effective number of species' interpretation is strained, and the sum pools all subplots+years (and bouts, per the bout finding) so a widespread species is weighted by ubiquity AND by being seen in more visits. (2) The evenness shown in hillInsight is q1/q0 (Shannon evenness, Pielou-like) — fine — but it's computed on this same non-partition. The math runs; the honesty caveat that's on the cover bar is NOT carried onto the Hill chart.
  - fix: Keep Hill (it's defensible as a relative-abundance diversity index) but (a) compute the per-species abundance as MEAN cover across sampled subplots (the structural-zero mean already in plot_species_cover), not a raw sum that conflates abundance with ubiquity/visit-count; (b) add the same 'cover is a relative ocular index, layers overlap' caveat that's on output$coverBar onto output$hillPlot via insight_banner or a chart subtitle, so the q1/q2 numbers travel with their caveat; (c) optionally offer an incidence-based diversity (Hill numbers on incidence frequencies) as the primary, since presence is the cleaner signal for plants and matches the Chao2 incidence framing.
- [medium/S] **Species-area curve computes a per-area sd then discards it — flagship Diversity chart has no uncertainty** (R/plant_helpers.R species_area_site() (returns sd, n); server.R output$saPlot)
  - problem: species_area_site() correctly computes sd and n per area_m2 (with the right ordering so sd isn't taken on the collapsed mean — that gotcha is handled). But output$saPlot draws only x=area_m2, y=richness as a bare lines+markers trace; the sd and n columns are never used. - [high/L] **Env-correlation feature (roadmap) will be data-dredging unless deseasonalization + a permutation null + matched-n ship with it** (roadmap ENVIRONMENT TAB; styles.css .ec/.env-pop-bar classes already ported; new helper needed in plant_helpers.R)
  - problem: The ported .ec banner and .env-pop-lag CSS imply a lag picker that scans plant metrics (cover, richness, % introduced over time) against monthly env drivers (precip_mm, temp_c, vswc_pct, rh_pct, fruiting_pct) and reports a big r-value. On NEON plant series this is the classic short-series multiple-comparisons trap: plant surveys are ~1/year (n often 4-10 site-years), env is monthly — so a scan over many lags x 5 drivers, taking max|r|, will return a large 'significant'-looking r under pure noise. See Vera for the .ec color: move the answer r off the native/introduced green-vs-cardinal poles onto a CVD-safe sign convention that can't be confused with nativity.

### Vik · performance
Vik here — profiled the reactive graph, the bundle sizes, and the precompute split. The architecture's bones are right (per-site .rds bundles read on demand, a precomputed site_index for the picker, lean manifest), but the render layer has zero caching: every output reads rv$snap/rv$occ and recomputes the heavy diversity math from raw rows on every fire, and the same expensive functions run 2-3x per single site load. The biggest single win is precomputing per-site analytics into the bundle (or a metrics.rds) so the app reads answers instead of recomputing them — this also de-risks the 46-site + env expansion, where the picker and any cross-site view must NOT scan 46 bundles. site_index is already the right shape but is under-populated and built by the wrong (mammal) script. Env data is tiny (~150KB for all 46) and should load lazily per-site, never at startup. Cold-start risk on a 1GB Connect Cloud worker is package load (plotly+leaflet+DT+sf-free is fine) plus the synchronous decompress+recompute on first site open; precompute kills most of it. No bindCache, no debounce, no reactive() memoization exist today — that's the headline.
