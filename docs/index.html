Read the signal honestly

Composition is not condition.

Plant richness is a useful description of community composition. It is not a shortcut to productivity, ecosystem health, or cause—and ocular cover is a relative index, not an exact fraction of ground.

✓ What this app can tell you

  • How observed richness grows from the 1 m² quadrat to the 400 m² plot.
  • Which native, introduced, and unknown-status taxa make up recorded cover.
  • Where a species or composition pattern appears in the bundled network.
  • How observed flora compares with an NRCS reference list, as completeness—not error.

! What it cannot settle

  • High richness does not prove high productivity, native function, or ecosystem health.
  • Introduced-cover values do not equal the percentage of ground lost to invasive plants.
  • A cross-scale occurrence pattern does not prove temporal spread or management priority.
  • Short per-site climate records do not establish a causal climate → vegetation link.

The survey is the story

Richness travels with its grain.

NEON’s nested design asks what is present as the search area expands. The app keeps those measured grains visible rather than blending them into one placeless number.

Cover is different: it comes from ocular estimates in the 1 m² quadrats. Plant layers overlap, so the app treats cover as a relative composition index.

Methods & data

Trace every summary back to the field design.

The explorer is built from NEON Plant Presence and Percent Cover data. Bundles are prepared ahead of time so site exploration is fast and does not depend on a live NEON download.

Snapshot discipline

Site metrics use the latest survey snapshot per plot rather than pooling repeat years into one inflated species list.

Richness estimate

Chao2 is an incidence-based lower-bound estimate of total richness; its difference from observed richness is an estimated unseen component, not a confirmed count. It is not a substitute for coverage-standardized cross-site comparison.

Composition index

Native, introduced, and unknown cover are calculated from represented 1 m² ocular observations, with species absences retained across known supported plots. The bundle cannot reconstruct sampled-but-empty quadrats.

Climate context

Short within-site records are exploratory. Permutation tests account for searching across drivers and lags.
